 Articles
 

It is the mission of this journal to engage in current debates that shape our understanding of cultural theory and religious studies, past, present, and future. The following "manifestoes" offer critical responses to recent issues in the field of religious studies – methodology, history, theory, and politics.

Weak Theology.
By Jeffrey W. Robbins, Lebanon Valley College.

Long Time Coming: Theology, Methodology, Cultural Theory.
By Clayton Crockett, University of Central Arkansas.

Theory Fever: Archivalism and the Future of Religious Studies.
By Victor E. Taylor, York College of Pennsylvania.

Radical Islam and the Specter of the Desert – otherwise, the Call of Theory.
By Carl Raschke, University of Denver.

Against Religion ("Without Religion"): A New Rationalist Reply to John D. Caputo's On Religion.
By Gregg Lambert, Syracuse University.

Love Among the Deconstructibles: A Response to Gregg Lambert.
By John D. Caputo, Villanova University.

The Return of the Prophet: A Dialogue.
Edited by Philip Goodchild, University of Nottingham.

Dialectic of the Past / Disjuncture of the Future: Derrida and Benjamin on the Concept of Messianism.
By Owen Ware, University of Victoria.

"A Philosophy and Theology of Hyperinflation": Walter Benjamin, Weimar and the New Thinking.
By Nikolas Lambrianou, Birkbeck, University of London.
